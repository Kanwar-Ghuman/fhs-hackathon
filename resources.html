Judging Rubrics

Coding Project Rubric

1. Problem Definition & Community Impact

Criteria: Clearly identifies a community issue and articulates how the solution will make a meaningful impact.

  • 1: Problem is vague or missing
  • 3: Problem is defined and has some impact
  • 5: Exceptionally clear, well-researched problem with strong potential for community enrichment

2. Innovation & Creativity

Criteria: Demonstrates originality and creative thinking in approach.

  • 1: Conventional solution with little originality
  • 3: Some creative elements evident
  • 5: Groundbreaking, highly inventive solution that stands out

3. Technical Execution & Functionality

Criteria: Code or app works reliably and doesn't crash.

  • 1: Major technical issues or unclear prototype
  • 3: Works as intended but with room for refinement
  • 5: Flawless, robust execution with a polished final product

4. Design & User Experience

Criteria: The design is intuitive, visually appealing, and user-friendly.

  • 1: Poor design; confusing or hard to use
  • 3: Adequate design with some room for improvement
  • 5: Highly engaging design that enhances usability and overall experience

5. Presentation & Communication

Criteria: Final presentation clearly explains the solution, research, and community benefit.

  • 1: Disorganized or unclear presentation
  • 3: Clear and structured but lacks some detail
  • 5: Exceptionally clear, engaging, and persuasive presentation

6. Feasibility & Scalability

Criteria: Solution is realistic with potential for growth.

  • 1: Not feasible; lacks implementation details
  • 3: Moderately feasible with some planning evident
  • 5: Highly practical and scalable with clear roadmap
